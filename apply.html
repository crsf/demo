How to apply for the Oxford-Canada Scholarship

Submit a written application by email to: ox-can.scholarship@rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk>. Your application must include:

  • Your resum√©
  • Scanned copy of your Oxford University academic transcript, complete or in progress (a copy of your secondary school performance would also be helpful)
  • Evidence of age (must be under 26 on 31 December in year of application) and UK Citizenship (birth certificate or passport in colour photocopy)
  • A personal statement
    • What you should include in your personal statement
  • Four references sent by your referees
    • What you need to do for your referees

Applications must be received prior to closing on 13 November 2020

Questions on process can be addressed by email to: ox-can.scholarship@rhodeshouse.ox.ac.uk

When to apply to a Canadian university

Approach the university of your choice (it would be preferable to approach more than one) before the selection process to ensure you have the required prerequisites for entry. Consider writing to the faculty head(s), if you have not yet identified a potential supervisor. An indication from a faculty member that, if accepted, that individual would supervise the student's work, would support the application for the scholarship.

There is no need to apply to the universities before applying for the Oxford-Canada scholarship but you should know when the universities open and close their applications periods (most Canadian universities open applications after the scholarship applications have closed).

Selection criteria

Oxford-Canada scholars are selected based on:

  • academic excellence
  • character
  • leadership potential, as demonstrated through extra-curricular involvement

Selection of Oxford-Canada Scholars is made without regard to gender, gender identity, marital status, sexual orientation, race, ethnic origin, colour, religion, social background, caste, or disability.
