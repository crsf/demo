Current scholars

Joe Cock

St. Catherine's College, Oxford, 2015

  • Research focus: Masters in Public and International Affairs
  • University: University of Ottawa

Mr. Cock, graduated from Oxford with first class honours in French and Linguistics. He is studying for a Masters in Public Affairs at the University of Ottawa. His studies have covered a wide range of disciplines in the field, with a specialisation in trade policy and, in particular, the implications of the Brexit for UK trade policy beyond the March 2019 negotiation deadline. As part of his MA course, this year, Mr. Cock is working for the political section of the US Embassy, in Ottawa. He thinks it most likely he will seek employment with the UK Government after graduation, although he has not ruled out a future in Canada. The photo was taken in Scheldt, British Columbia, with the spoils of his first-ever fishing trip. He made the journey with the scholarship travel grant.

Prashant Pandey

Brasenose College, Oxford, 2016

  • Research focus: PhD in Biomedical Engineering
  • University: University of British Columbia

Mr. Pandey graduated with a Master's in Engineering. He is pursuing a PhD in Biomedical Engineering at the University of British Columbia (UBC). His research is in the field of medical imaging and computer vision, developing a surgical navigation system to guide doctors during pelvic fracture surgeries. Mr. Pandey chose UBC for his graduate studies because of its emphasis on collaborative training and research. He considers himself fortunate to work closely with surgeons on clinical problems, which is unlike most engineering programs. He says his future is committed to solving real problems, to the benefit patients and clinicians alike.

Rebecca Pearse

Queen's College, Oxford, 2017

  • Research focus: Masters in Neuroscience
  • University: McGill University

Ms Pearse graduated from Oxford with a contratulatory first in Experimental Psychology. She will study for a Masters in Neuroscience, at McGill University. There, she says she looks forward to working with world-class supervisors, in the areas of language cognition and addiction therapy, making use of McGill's large range and high quality laboratories. She adds that she looks forward to living in the cosmopolitan, bilingual and culturally exciting city of Montreal. After her Masters, she plans to work toward a PhD in Neuroscience, in Canada or elsewhere.

Anna Seccombe

Wadham College, Oxford, 2017

  • Research focus: Masters in History
  • University: University of Toronto

Ms Seccombe graduated from Oxford with first-class honours for a joint degree in History and English.

She is studying for a Master’s degree in History at the University of Toronto, working on the theory and sociology of diasporas. Her research will be on the politics of nationalism among immigrants to Canada in the mid- to late twentieth century, for which she looks forward to interviewing Ukrainians, in Toronto. She write that she is excited to make use of all the resources that the University of Toronto has to offer, to learn in an environment with such a global outlook, and to live in one of the most diverse and international cities in the world.

In the future, she would like to work for an international non-profit and apply her research skills to policy issues concerning migration.
